Privacy notice

How Nomika Studio uses your email information

Last updated: July 2026

Information collected

When you request the free Excel tracker, Nomika Studio collects the email address you submit through the signup form. MailerLite may also process technical information needed to operate the form, confirm consent and measure email delivery or engagement.

Why the information is collected

Your email address is used to:

  • send the requested free Excel tracker;
  • send helpful Excel and business-organisation tips;
  • send occasional Nomika Studio product or freelance-service updates;
  • maintain the security and performance of the email list.

Consent and double opt-in

You are added to the active list only after completing the signup form and confirming your email through the double-opt-in message. You may withdraw consent at any time by using the unsubscribe link in a marketing email.

Email service provider

Nomika Studio uses MailerLite to collect subscribers and deliver emails. MailerLite processes subscriber information on behalf of Nomika Studio according to its own privacy and security terms.

Data sharing

Nomika Studio does not sell your email address to advertisers. Information may be processed by service providers that are necessary to operate the signup form, email delivery, hosting and security.

Retention and deletion

Your email information is retained while you remain subscribed or while it is reasonably necessary to maintain records of consent, comply with applicable requirements, resolve disputes or protect the service. You may request deletion by contacting Nomika Studio.

Your choices

You may unsubscribe from marketing emails at any time. You may also ask to access, correct or delete the personal information held about you, subject to applicable requirements.

Contact

Questions or privacy requests can be sent to hello.nomika@gmail.com.

This notice is a practical business template and is not legal advice. Nomika Studio should review it when its services, data practices or legal obligations change.
